In a nut-shell

In this project, we present case studies of distance metrics, (standard/traditional and state-of-the-art) on unstructured data, particularly from E-commerce domain. The project focuses on graph-based semi-supervised learning (SSL) algorithms, which are traditionally popular among graph structured datasets, such as online social networks et cetera (Subramanya et al. 2014), however, researchers and practitioners are beginning to realise that graph can be used to represent data in an organic way (Wu et al. 2018). Although extensive research has been done in the classification sub-problem of the graph-based SSL techniques (Peel 2016, Talukdar et al. 2009), the graph construction sub-problem remains relatively untouched. Distance metric such as cosine similarity (CS) is shown to be effective for quantifying text similarity in the case of unstructured data, which can be used to construct a graph for data domains such as E-commerce (E-com.). Particularly, state-of-the-art metric inverse sqrt-cosine (ISC) similarity is shown to be effective (Sohangir et al. 2017) for text based data. We present a study on how the distance/similarity metrics impact the graph construction and the subsequent classification task in cases of unstructured text data...
